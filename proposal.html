ManiMAtCha

by Thien Le, Wen Qi, William Ling, Zekai Wang

Problem Description

Our project combines MAtCha’s chart-based rendering with mesh-level optimization inspired by NeuManifold to produce high-quality meshes output with less input image needed. We aim to bridge these two methods through a shared pipeline that allows for differentiable mesh loss and improved geometry reconstruction.

Our goal is to explore what happens when we combine the strengths of MAtCha Gaussians and NeuManifold. MAtCha’s pipeline has two main parts: it first aligns a series of monocular depth maps from sparse-view images to create a rough 3D surface (a manifold of charts), and then places and optimizes 2D Gaussians along those surfaces for photorealistic rendering. While MAtCha excels at generating good visual quality from just a few images, it doesn't guarantee clean, watertight meshes. We’re building on top of this by adding mesh-level optimization inspired by NeuManifold—specifically, introducing mesh-aware losses and potentially backpropagating through the mesh itself. The idea is to keep MAtCha’s efficient rendering and surface recovery while improving geometry quality, aiming for a final result that’s both photorealistic and mesh-usable for applications like editing, simulation, or 3D printing.

Our approach involves adding an additional loss based on the geometry of the generated mesh itself. To do that, we can incorporate NeuManifold’s differentiable marching cubes algorithm, which allows backpropagation through the mesh. The challenge is figuring out how to connect the MAtCha pipeline with NeuManifold’s mesh-based optimization.
