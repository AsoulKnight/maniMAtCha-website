ManiMAtCha

CS184 Midway Milestone Report

Due: April 20, 2025

Accomplishments to Date

We’re combining MAtCha’s chart-based rendering with mesh-level optimization techniques inspired by NeuManifold. The goal is to produce high-quality mesh outputs using fewer input images.

Preliminary Results

So far, we’ve been working on integrating chart outputs with a differentiable Marching Cubes algorithm. We’ve finally gotten it running, and the results are looking great—clean meshes and even some early experiments with colorization, similar to Blender-style visualizations.

Reflection on Progress

The key idea is to bridge MAtCha and mesh optimization through a unified pipeline that enables differentiable mesh loss. This should allow us to better reconstruct geometry and make the pipeline more end-to-end trainable.

We’ve also started running mesh evaluations on our dataset and will be expanding that soon. Next steps include designing the training pipeline and creating an interface to visualize the effects of different variables and inputs.

Updated Work Plan

  • Week 3 (Apr 21–28): Complete mesh‑aware loss and test on simple scenes.
  • Week 4 (Apr 28–May 5): Optimize joint pipeline, finalize visuals, and prepare final presentation.
